Field Calibration Guide
Validate and tune measurement accuracy against a tape measure.
Step 1 — Find a reference
Pick something with a known real-world width at the base of your photo frame — a standard floor tile (300 mm or 600 mm), a road marking, or a kerb section you've measured with tape.
Step 2 — Set the lines
Position the Building edge and Kerb edge lines so they align exactly with the edges of your reference object at the bottom of the frame. Lock both.
Step 3 — Calibrate
Tap the Scale badge → enter the actual width of your reference → tap Apply Calibration. The scale factor updates and is saved for your phone model.
Expected accuracy
After one calibration, expect ±5–15% accuracy on level footpaths in good light. Accuracy improves when the footpath fills the lower half of the frame and lines are locked at the bottom edge.
Tips for best results
• Hold phone at consistent height (elbow height, ~1.2 m)
• Point camera straight ahead, not angled down
• Both edges of the footpath should be visible
• Lock the focal point once set for a survey session
• Zoom in to align lines precisely at the bottom of frame
